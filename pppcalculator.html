What is Purchase Power Parity?

Purchasing power parity (PPP) conversion factor is a spatial price deflator and currency converter that controls for price level differences between countries, thereby allowing volume comparisons of gross domestic product (GDP) and its expenditure components. This conversion factor is for GDP. To know more, click on the "Learn More" button below.


Purchase Power Parity Calculator

Understanding Purchase Power Parity (PPP):

Purchase Power Parity is a measure of the relative value of currencies. This calculator uses PPP to adjust your salary based on the cost of living in different countries, giving you insights into your potential purchasing power abroad.

Currency Conversion Example:

If you're moving from a country with a high cost of living to one with a lower cost of living, your adjusted salary might allow you to lead a more comfortable life.

Why is this calculator needed?

This calculator allows you to understand the relative purchasing power of salaries in different countries, helping you make informed decisions about living and working internationally.

How to use the calculator:

1. Select your country.

2. Enter your salary.

3. Choose a country to compare.

4. Click "Calculate" to see the adjusted salary based on purchasing power parity.

Example: Currency Conversion

Suppose you are considering a job offer in the United States and want to compare your salary with your home country, let's say India.

1. Select "United States" as your country and "India" as the comparison country.

2. Enter your salary in the provided field.

3. Click "Calculate" to see the adjusted salary based on purchasing power parity.

This way, you can understand how your salary would fare in terms of purchasing power in a different country.
